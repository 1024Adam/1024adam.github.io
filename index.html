The Co-operators

About the Company

The Co-operators, is an insurance company that was formed in Canada in 1945. It was originally built by farmers to take care of themselves because traditional insurers did not meet their needs at the time. They provide a number of different services to this day, such as auto, home, and commercial insurance. Currently, The Co-operators have over a million clients across the Canada. I worked at the Co-operators head office, which is located in downtown Guelph, Ontario.

About My Role

My main job at The Co-operators was as a Quality Assurance Analyst. I worked on a QA team of testers, along with the entire document solutions team, to ensure that all of the documents produced from the system were accurate, and correct. The system that produces these documents is called Guidewire PolicyCenter, so my main job was to ensure that all of the information that was entered in PolicyCenter correctly printed on the documents that will go out towards their clients. During my time there, our team thoroughly tested all aspect of the Automotive insurance documents, performing many different types of tests to do so, including system integration testing, regression testing, black box testing, and white box testing. Near the end of my term, I was asked to look into the possibility of using an program called Selenium to automate our testing process during the regression stages. By the end of my term, I had successfully created an automation script that simulates a policy being created using a set of data in an Excel document. This script will be used in the future, as they develop further into this program, and the benefits it can have on the team’s testing process.

Another task that I was assigned to work on was the testing of a software upgrade called Prinova MessagePoint. This software was used, along with their Guidewire software, to produce the documents that my team reviewed. I was trusted to individually test this upgraded software, in order to ensure that it did not interfere with the correct composition of the policy documents. The testing for this upgrade took a total of about a month, which consisted of gathering information about the upgrade, gathering test samples, and performing all required test cases that I had created to be sure that the entire system worked with no difference to the previously upgraded one. By the end of the testing period, I successfully reported any and all defects found in my testing, had them fixed by the Prinova developers, and confirmed that all errors have been fixed.

University of Guelph

About the Company

Hospitality Services is the department in the University of Guelph, and is a recognized leader for quality and innovation in Canadian University food services. The Hospitality Services IT department deals with a variety of different jobs, including ensuring that meal cards are working, and processing transactions made on a daily basis. They offer many different plans to accommodate both students, and faculty, which definitely makes it a very significant department within the University of Guelph.

About My Role

One thing that was particularly different about the role I had was that I was the sole individual responsible for both the development and testing that I completed in my work term. Being in this situation provided me with a lot of work to do, and a lot of responsibility as well. This helped me to improve my individual working skills, as many of the issues I ran into I had to solve on my own, rather than relying on more experienced developers for assistance. This was actually one of the aspects of the job that I liked the most, as it helped me to be able to solve problems on my own much faster, and more efficiently.

The first job that I was tasked with was to re-configure, and improve their online price book management system. This is a web application, which had stopped working when they made upgrades to their server. The website was originally developed in PHP, which coming into this work term I had minimal experience with. This task took approximately a month to fully complete. My main task completed was to re-configure the system so then it functioned properly again. Also, I implemented many administration functions such as adding revenue centre groups, and deactivating revenue centres. This definitely was a great learning experience for me. I learned a lot about the language of PHP, and what its capabilities are as a language. This task also helped me to improve my problem solving skills, which was accomplished by restoring the system back to its functioning state.

My next task, which was my main task for the work term, was to re-implement their Online Services website. The Online Services website provides users an interface to purchase accounts, add money to accounts, cancel accounts, and a variety of other features. This website used to be developed in a ColdFusion environment, which was troublesome, as the ColdFusion servers were very unstable, and would go down a lot of the time. My job was to convert this ColdFusion code, into a .NET environment. I would say that the most difficult task in doing this was understanding what the ColdFusion code was doing, and attempting to find an equivalent function in C#, which was the language that I was using to develop the .NET application. During this process, I found that the ColdFusion language itself is very high level, and that many functions which are only one or two lines in ColdFusion can take up to twenty lines in other, more commonly used languages. I was also able to automate some of the features that used to be accomplished by a manual process. One specific function was the transfer of funds component of the system. Originally this process consisted of a request to transfer funds being sent to the administrator, and they would go into the system and complete the transaction manually. Now, the users can do this automatically through the website, which definitely saves administration a lot of time as a result. I would say the most important thing that I learned in completing this task is the ability to convert an entire system over into another language. This required me to have a lot of problem solving skills, and to obtain a lot of knowledge in both the ColdFusion, and in C# languages.

 

My final tasks for my work term consisted of converting other, smaller applications within the Hospitality Services department over into .NET environments as well. These are all applications that were originally developed in ColdFusion or PHP, and needed to be converted to C# to be able to place them in the .NET environment. Some of these applications needed to be rewritten entirely however, such as their Red Top application used to process transactions by the Red Top taxi service. Completing these kinds of tasks was definitely a nice change, as this was the first time in my work term that I wasn’t working with a preexisting website. Instead, I got to develop these specific applications from scratch, which I quite enjoyed.

The last goal that I set to accomplish was to independently handle, and lead the development of all of the applications that I produced in my work term. Brock Solutions

About the Company

Brock Solutions is a company that provides a variety of engineering solutions and services to their clients. They operate under three main sectors: transportation and logistics, manufacturing, and panel fabrication services. Brock has over 400 employees in both Canada and the United States, and has been in business for over 30 years. Brock has been a continual winner of the Canada’s 50 Best Managed Company Awards since 1996. They also provide sponsorships for numerous Brock sports teams such as slo-pitch, hockey, and volleyball. I worked in one of their main offices, which is located in Kitchener, Ontario.

About My Role

My role in Brock Solutions was to be a Software Developer. Initially, I was the main developer, and worked alongside my project manager Ryan. In this circumstance, I would provide the majority of development services, and Ryan would help out with minor additions, and conduct code reviews on the work that I had done to ensure that it was meeting Brock’s coding standards. The project that I worked on was to make an operations management system for one of the clients of Brock. The purpose of this system was to keep track of scheduling, employee statistics, and equipment status, as well as allow users to configure employee and equipment information. During this part of my work term, I was able to become more comfortable with my independent working skills, as most of my work did not involve interacting with other co-workers, other than my manager when I needed assistance.

The software development tool that was used to make this type of application is called Ignition. Ignition allows for developers to design a Java-like application, using a ‘drag-and-drop’ like UI development style for the front-end, and using scripting languages for the back-end. The scripting in the back-end was mainly done in a language called Jython, which is Python for the most part, but also includes the ability to incorporate Java libraries if needed. Ignition makes it very easy to rapidly develop complex applications, which is beneficial especially when in a small timeframe to deliver a product. Using this tool also allows for developers to incorporate pre-built modules made for Ignition, which provide services such as data recording, reporting, and alarming. Along with using Ignition for the front-end and back-end work, the project also involved using SQL for data recording and querying. I definitely learned a lot more about SQL than I had previously known from the start of this term, as the complexity of queries that I had to write was at a much higher level than I had ever needed to know in previous work terms and in school.

Closer to the end of my work term, the number of Ignition projects that my team was involved with increased. These types of projects were similar to that of the first project I worked on, where clients required a type of management application for their employees or their equipment. To be able to complete these projects on time, the team I was on grew to include more developers. During this part of my work term, I was also used as a resource to new developers. Since I had spent the majority of my work term using Ignition, I knew a lot more about what capabilities it had. With this knowledge, I feel that I was able to decrease the time it took to get new team members comfortable with Ignition, which allowed them to start developing almost immediately. Working with more team members definitely helped me to improve my teamwork and communication skills, as I was consistently helping co-workers to help solve any of their problems that they were having.

 

 

Brock Solutions

My Goals

Since I was in an eight-month co-op work term, I made four goals that I was to accomplish for each four-month term that I had, for a total of eight goals. In doing this I felt like I would be able to cover a wider variety of goals. Furthermore, after my first four months, I would have a better idea of the type of work I would be doing. This would allow me to make goals that are directed more at that specific type of work.

For the first four months, one of my goals was to further my knowledge in programming in at least one of the languages that was used at Brock Solutions. Coming into the job, I knew that they used Java, and I had known when making this goal that I would be working with Python as well, so I decided that I would work towards learning more about those languages. I felt that doing this would help me to become a better developer, which would also help me to acquire future jobs that dealt with similar types of languages. While completing this goal, I was researching online, and consulting Brock resources to improve my knowledge of the two languages. By the end of my four months, I was consistently implementing my own code into the applications that I had made, as well as previously made projects that needed modifications.

One skill that I felt would be very beneficial for me to work towards improving on while at Brock was to improve my teamwork skills. For the most part, in both school and previous work terms, I did not have that much experience working with groups on projects. Completing this goal would not only mean that I would have more skill working with others, but it would also improve my communication skills with team members as well. During the first four months, I was not able work on many larger teams. However, Brock offers a variety of different social activities, such as weekly basketball, barbeques, and team ‘lunch and learns’, which I felt really helped me to get comfortable around other co-workers who I did not normally work with on a regular basis. Especially with basketball, where teamwork is crucial in a team’s success, I felt that I was able to definitely improve on this skill, which can be applied into the work environment as well.

Considering that I knew I would be working with the Ignition software tool for the majority of my co-op term, I felt that it would be in my best interest to make it a goal of mine to learn as much as I could about the software. One of the first things that helped with this was that Inductive Automation, the company that developed Ignition, offers online courses which included videos and evaluations that test your knowledge of the software. The first couple weeks of my work term was almost completely dedicated to completing all of the courses that Inductive Automation had to offer. That, combined with the fact that I was using the software on a regular basis, really helped me to solidify my knowledge of the software. By the end of my first four months, I knew more about the software and what its capabilities were, which allowed for me to make better decisions in terms of the design of the applications that I was going to be making. This also helped the team later on, when new developers were joining. I was able to provide to them useful resources that I had found that would be most relevant to the work that they would be doing.

A goal that I am always continuing to try and improve is my written communication skills. Specifically, for the start of my work term, I wanted to try and get better at communicating the type of work that I was completing. In doing this, I would be able to better describe to customers the different aspects of the work that I do, which will give them more confidence that my team is properly completing the projects they have for us. Each week when submitting timesheets, I was required to write a sentence for each of the different tasks that I completed the previous week. Furthermore, for each project-related document that I wrote, I would review it to ensure that what I wrote matched exactly with I was trying to explain. I would also comment my code in areas where there may be some confusion, so then both myself and other developers can look at the code and immediately tell what function it is completing. This is definitely a skill that I feel improved over my entire work term, and will be useful in all aspects of my life, including future jobs that I have.

During the second half of my work term, one skill that I wanted to improve on was my ability to write quality requirement documents and specifications. In the four months that had passed, I had realized that I was not very comfortable producing these types of documents, and I knew that I would need to improve this skill in order to succeed in this position. The feedback that I obtained from my manager and fellow team members really helped me to identify key areas of improvement for me, which I worked to improve on by the end of my work term. I also took the time to look at previous requirements documents that have been made by Brock, to see if there was anything that I have been missing in my documents. I have produced many different types of specification documents since making this goal, and have had them both self-reviewed and peer-reviewed to ensure that they were correct and appropriate to share with our customers.

Something I chose to continue working towards improving throughout the rest of my entire eight-month work term was to improve my teamwork skills. Since I was rarely on a team with more than two people for the first half of the term, I thought it would be best to continue working towards completing this goal, especially with respect to working with a larger team. Thankfully, in the second half of my term the team I was on grew to about seven people, with most of them being co-workers I had no experience working with. Each day, we participated in scrum meetings to discuss what we were working on, and noting any potential roadblocks that might be a concern. On a consistent basis, I provided my opinions and thoughts towards possible solutions to problems or concerns that other team members had. Outside of work, I continued to participate in sporting events with Brock employees, such as basketball, volleyball, and ping pong.

Since by this time I had already developed a solid understanding of the Ignition software, it made sense for me to look toward extending my knowledge of the tool. To do this, I decided to look into external, and third party modules that can be imported and used in Ignition. I conducted research into a variety of tools, such as a barcode scanner, downtime, and production related modules. For each one that I researched, I produced an analysis document, which outlined the functionality and capabilities of the modules, and concluding if the module can be used in any current projects. Along with that, I would try to extend the functionality of Ignition by looking into unconventional ways to complete complicated tasks. An example of this is with customized pop-up windows that are synchronous to the main windows in Ignition. To try and overcome the inability to complete this using basic techniques, I would look into Ignition functions that could be utilized in a certain way to complete this task effectively. At the end of my co-op term, I had felt that I learned most, if not all of what could be accomplished using Ignition, including what can be done with third party modules.

One skill that I had struggled to improve on during previous work terms was to work on my oral communication skills, specifically directed at presenting what I have done to groups of people. Leading presentations has always been something that I have been uncomfortable doing in the past. I also knew that to be able to succeed as an employee at Brock, I would need to improve on this skill to be able to adequately present applications to clients and potential customers. During the second half of my term, I was the main presenter in two factory acceptance tests, one of which I got to travel on-site and present to the customer face-to-face. These types of presentations lasted anywhere from three to four hours. Furthermore, at the end of my co-op, the other students and I presented information about what we worked on at Brock. This was done over Skype, and was shared with over forty employees that work at Brock. I not only believe that I have become more comfortable with leading presentations, but I have also improved the quality of my presentations, which was my ideal outcome of this goal.

Overall, the goals that I set to accomplish allowed me to have great success in this job. My fellow team members were all very supportive, provided help, and gave knowledge whenever possible. These goals will hopefully be able to help me not only with jobs that I have in the future, but possibly in school as well.
